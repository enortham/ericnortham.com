Eric Northam

Builder, founder, and problem solver based in Mexico City

Who I Am

I've been living in Mexico City since 2012, where I'm raising my 12-year-old and staying active with calisthenics on gymnastic rings. Originally from Washington DC, I've been abroad since 2005—six years in Buenos Aires, two years traveling the world, and now deeply rooted here in Mexico.

I studied Computer Science at UMBC and Takushoku University in Tokyo, where I eventually learned to speak Japanese well (and then subsequently forgot it). I still love to program, mainly in Ruby, but I'm equally passionate about design, product, entrepreneurship and writing.

What I'm Building

I'm the CEO and Founder of EasyBroker, which I started in 2007. We've built Mexico's leading Real Estate CRM, 100% bootstrapped, never taking outside investment.

I came to Mexico because EasyBroker took off here, and I wanted to be closer to my clients and truly understand the market. Recently, we launched Pincali, a real estate portal with an ambitious goal: to become the leading source for finding homes to buy or rent in Mexico.

Why It Matters

I believe in building solutions for important problems, especially in emerging markets where technology can transform traditional industries. Real estate in Mexico needed better tools, and we're building them. There's something deeply satisfying about creating sustainable, bootstrapped businesses that actually solve problems for people.
